## **Hashing Explained Like I'm 5 (But for Beginners!)**

Imagine you have a **toy box with 10 drawers**, numbered 0 to 9. You want to store your toys (data) so you can find them super fast.

---

### **The Magic Rule (Hash Function):**
You make a simple rule: **"Count the letters in the toy's name, then use the last digit."**

Examples:
- **"Ball"** â†’ 4 letters â†’ last digit is **4** â†’ goes in drawer **4**

---

### **What Happens When Two Toys Go to Same Drawer?**
#### **Option 1: Pile them up!** (Separate Chaining)
- Drawer 4 has "Ball", so you just put "Doll" in the same drawer
- Like stacking books in one shelf

#### **Option 2: Find next empty drawer** (Open Addressing)
- Drawer 4 is full? Check drawer 5
- Drawer 5 full? Check drawer 6, and so on...

---

### **Why is This Cool?**
Instead of searching **ALL drawers** for "Car", you:
1. Count letters in "Car" = 3
